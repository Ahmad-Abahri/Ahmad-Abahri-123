Palestine over the years


The land of Palestine witnessed many occupations on its lands in different years, and some of these occupations :

  • British Mandate

    • On September 11, 1922, the League of Nations officially approved the mandate, by issuing a deed entrusting the administration of Palestine to Britain; This is based on the agreement of the Allied countries to implement a British royal declaration issued in 1917, known as the Balfour Declaration, and it was mentioned in more than one place in the Declaration of the League of Nations, an affirmation not to take action that would harm the civil and religious rights of the indigenous people of Palestine and the preservation of their rights, in line with the encouragement of cooperation with The Zionist Association to facilitate the immigration of Jews to Palestine and to acquire Palestinian citizenship for those immigrants, as a guarantee for the establishment of a homeland A Jewish national for them, according to the text of the decision. The resolution defined the mandate area for Palestine, including Transjordan, but Article 25 of it allowed for the postponement or cessation of the application of texts to it, and it was actually excluded from the mandate in 1921 according to the Transjordan Memorandum, so the Emirate of Transjordan enjoyed self-rule and was not subject to the principles of the mandate or the Balfour Declaration . The Emirate of Transjordan became independent from British authority in 1946 to become the Kingdom of Jordan, and thus it was de facto excluded from the borders of the mandate over Palestine, so the mandate over Palestine remained between the Jordan River and the Mediterranean until the end of the mandate in 1948, and those are the borders of Palestine in which both the State of Israel and the West Bank are located today. West and the Gaza Strip, The city of Jerusalem was the capital of the Mandate, where the British governor and the institutions of the Mandate government resided. At the beginning of the Mandate period, Britain declared a goal to achieve the Balfour Declaration, i.e. open the door for Jews wishing to emigrate to Palestine and establish a Jewish “national home” there. In the mid-thirties of the twentieth century, Britain changed its policy and tried to stop the influx of Jews into Palestine and prevent the purchase of land by the Jews.

  • The Nakba

    • is a Palestinian term that deals with the human tragedy related to the displacement of a large number of the Palestinian people outside their homes. It is the name given by the Palestinians to their displacement and the demolition of most of the political, economic and cultural features of their society in 1948. It was the year in which the Palestinian people were expelled from their home and land and lost their homeland, in favor of establishing the Jewish state - Israel. The events of the Nakba include the occupation of most of the lands of Palestine by the Zionist movement, and the expulsion of more than 750 thousand Palestinians and turn them into refugees. And the expulsion of most of the Bedouin tribes that destroyed the Palestinian identity and attempts to destroy the neighboring identity of the geographical projects and based on the ethnic cleansing began in 1947 after the proposal of the United Nations resolution to partition Palestine. The absolute Zionist gangs began trying to implement the matter on the ground

  • The 1967 war

    • also known in both Syria and Jordan as the June setback, and in Egypt as the 67 setback, and in Israel is called the Six-Day War is the war that broke out between Israel and each of Iraq, Egypt, Syria and Jordan between 5 June 1967 and the tenth of the same month, which led to Israel's occupation of Sinai, the Gaza Strip, the West Bank, and the Golan Heights, and is considered the third war within the Arab-Israeli conflict; The war led to the deaths of 15,000-25,000 people in the Arab countries 800 in Israel, and the destruction of 70-80% of the war materiel in the Arab countries, compared to 2-5% in Israel, along with a similar disparity in the number of wounded and prisoners; Among its results was the issuance of Security Council Resolution No. 242, the convening of the Arab Three No’s Summit in Khartoum, the displacement of most of the residents of the cities of the Suez Canal, the displacement of most of the civilians of Quneitra Governorate in Syria, the displacement of tens of thousands of Palestinians from the West Bank, including the erasure of entire villages, and the opening of the door to settlement in Jerusalem. East and West Bank. The consequences of the 1967 war have not ended until today, as Israel still occupies the West Bank, and it annexed Jerusalem and the Golan to its borders, and one of its consequences was also the outbreak of the October War in 1973 and the separation of the West Bank from Jordanian sovereignty, and the acceptance of the Arabs since the Madrid Peace Conference in 1991 with the principle of « Land in exchange for peace,” which provides for a return to the pre-war borders in exchange for Arab recognition of Israel, and peace with it; Although many Arab countries are establishing separate relations with Israel, politically or economically.
